FAQ: WHAT DOES EACH METRIC MEAN?

Q: "What is Node Purity?"
A: Fraction of samples in a topological region belonging to the dominant subject
   - Purity 1.0 = perfect separation (only 1 subject)
   - Purity 0.5 = mixed (equal subjects)
   
Q: "Why is average purity 59.6% and not higher?"
A: Normal! Overlapping cubes in Mapper intentionally mix samples to capture
   topology. High-purity regions still exist for authentication.

Q: "Should I reject the 20% very-low-confidence nodes?"
A: YES. Deploy only ≥0.7 purity for security, ≥0.5 for general use.

Q: "Is 24% high-confidence coverage enough?"
A: For proof-of-concept: YES. For production: scale to 1000+ subjects first.

Q: "How does this compare to traditional fingerprint matching?"
A: Traditional: Fast, less interpretable, needs minutiae extraction
   TDA: Slower, highly interpretable, global topology preservation
